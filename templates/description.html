How it works

Phoneme Search is quite easy to use. Basically you give your search input, which then is searched for. There are three ways to define phonemes or groups of phonemes:

1: Your input is merely an individual grapheme, like 'a'. In most cases the relation between a sign you put in and the phoneme which is searched for is obvious. For example the 'a' mentioned before means 'a' in the languages. Some phonemes are written with two characters, like 'aspirated t' in Vedic and Greek.

2: Another method is to use simple key words which resemble certain groups of phonemes, ordered by phonemic features. These keys are capital letters. For example 'P' stands for plosive therefore all plosive phonemes are allowed at the place where you put it into your pattern. These abbreviations can be combined with the '+'-character. If you write 'P+L' all plosive labials are allowed. There are four categories: manner, place, aspiration and voice. Each of these can be combined to a single cluster.
